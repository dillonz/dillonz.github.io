Smash Racer

Tech Stack: Unity, C#

Link to Windows Build: Smash Racer

I had two weeks to solo create a game in my class, and the only specifications was that it had to be a game with a unique mechanic. In this case, my unique mechanic was a sideways dash that could destroy other cars in a never-ending racing game. It's a fairly straightforward game, and it was designed to feel very fast and out of control by the time you die.

Throughout the two weeks, I only had one time of outside feedback from my class, which was in the middle of the two weeks. From that feedback, I had to iterate on my design and improve the game and interestingness of the mechanic. One major bit of feedback I got was that performance wasn't great on many people's machines, so I had to adjust lighting and after effects until it was stable and running smoothly on even a potato. Unfortunately, due to the time crunch, this meant some of the lighting felt off in the end, but the usability was much, much better.

Some key systems I had to build:

  • An autopilot mechanism both for enemies and for the player when they are in the shop
  • Endless road generation so that it never hits itself and appears like one seemless road
  • Generating and managing coins, light pillars, and enemies such that they always appear on the track and have good, yet increasing, spawn rates as the player continues
  • Lots of "juice" so that dashing into enemies, using powerups, entering/exiting the shop, taking damage, and general movement feels good and immersive to the player
  • All art assets, created using blender (which I learned for this project), and paint programs.
  • Increasing speed and difficulty of the game at a steady pace such that the player doesn't feel too difficult of a spike, but the game still gets more difficult.
